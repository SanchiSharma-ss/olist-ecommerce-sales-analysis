# Brazilian E-Commerce Analysis (Olist)

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist to understand sales trends, customer behavior, product performance, and geographic distribution.

Using Python (Pandas, Matplotlib) and Power BI, the project explores nearly 100,000 e-commerce orders and provides actionable business recommendations based on the findings.

The analysis focuses on answering key business questions:

Which product categories generate the highest revenue?
Which categories sell the highest volume?
How concentrated is revenue across products?
Which states contribute the most revenue and customers?
How satisfied are customers?
How frequently do customers make repeat purchases?

## Key Findings

### Sales Analysis

* Revenue grew steadily throughout 2017 and 2018, with November 2017 emerging as the strongest month in terms of both sales and order volume.
* Average Order Value remained fairly consistent over time, suggesting stable customer spending patterns.
* A significant portion of revenue came from a relatively small set of products and regions.

### Product Analysis
* Health Beauty was the highest revenue-generating category, while Bed Bath Table recorded the highest number of units sold.
* Categories with higher-priced products were able to generate strong revenue despite lower sales volumes.
* The top 10 product categories contributed more than 62% of total revenue, showing that sales were concentrated among a few key categories.

### Customer Analysis
* Customers were generally satisfied with their purchases, with an average review score of 4.09 and a large share of 5-star ratings.
* Repeat purchases were relatively uncommon, with only 3.12% of customers placing more than one order.
* On average, customers placed just over one order, indicating that business growth depended more on attracting new customers than retaining existing ones.

### Geographic Analysis
* São Paulo was the largest market, accounting for nearly 37% of total revenue and having the highest customer base.
* Revenue and customer distribution were heavily concentrated in the Southeast region.
* The top three states contributed roughly 64% of total revenue.
* States with larger customer bases generally generated higher revenue, highlighting a strong relationship between customer concentration and sales performance.

### Business Recommendations
* Focus on improving customer retention through loyalty programs, personalized offers, and post-purchase engagement.
* Continue investing in high-performing categories such as Health Beauty and Bed Bath Table, which drive a large share of sales.
* Explore opportunities in emerging markets to diversify revenue and reduce dependence on a few states.
* Leverage high customer satisfaction levels to encourage repeat purchases and increase customer lifetime value.
* Expand marketing efforts beyond the top performing regions to create a more balanced and resilient customer base.
